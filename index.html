I am a PhD student in the Polley Lab at Harvard University. Before moving to Boston for graduate school, I did my undergraduate studies at Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Madras in India where I majored in Electrical Engineering and minored in Biomedical Engineering. While I mostly study brains, I also ocassionaly fly planes, and my love for nature and adventure never wanes! :)

For my doctoral thesis, I have studied how the neural coding of sound transforms along the auditory neuro-axis, the plasticity in the neural coding after hearing loss, and sensory guided decision making in challenging environments. To this end, I've used extracellular electrophysiology to record neural activity simultaneously from multiple regions in the auditory neuro-axis of awake mice, as well as calcium imaging to track changes in cell-type specific activity across multiple days. Going forward, I'm broadly interested in studying how our perception of the sensory environment and our decision making changes in different emotional and motivational contexts.
